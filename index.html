What is Semantic HTML?

Semantic HTML means using HTML tags that accurately describe the meaning and structure of the content they contain, rather than just for presentation. For example, using <h1> for the main title and <p> for a paragraph tells a browser (and a screen reader) exactly what that content is.

Before HTML5, developers often used generic <div> tags for everything, like <div id="header">. Now, we have dedicated tags like <header>, <nav>, and <main> that give our content inherent meaning.

Why It's a Big Deal

For Accessibility (a11y)

For people who use screen readers, a well-structured semantic page is like a well-labeled map. They can easily jump between the header, main content, and footer instead of having to listen to every single word on the page to find what they're looking for.
